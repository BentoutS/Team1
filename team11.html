Our Mission & Vision

The Advanced Biomathematics Innovation (ABI) team is dedicated to pioneering mathematical and computational approaches to unravel biological complexity. Our mission is to develop transformative models that predict, understand, and ultimately mitigate challenges in public health, ecological sustainability, and the fundamental dynamics of living systems. We foster an interdisciplinary environment where rigorous theory meets real-world data to drive scientific discovery and inform policy.

Team Overview

The Advanced Biomathematics Innovation (ABI) group operates at the vanguard of interdisciplinary research, synergizing sophisticated mathematical modeling with deep biological insights. Our collective expertise spans the development and analysis of advanced mathematical frameworks, including ordinary and partial differential equations (ODE/PDEs), fractional calculus, and stochastic processes. These are meticulously applied to dissect complex phenomena in critical domains such as epidemiology, systems ecology, and the intrinsic dynamics of biological populations. We are committed to a research paradigm that not only advances theoretical understanding but also translates into tangible applications, addressing pressing real-world challenges through robust, data-informed models and computational simulations.

Core Research Pillars

Dynamical Systems in Biology

We develop and analyze deterministic and stochastic ODE/PDE frameworks to capture the intricate dynamics of biological processes. Our applications focus on elucidating mechanisms in disease transmission, population fluctuations, and ecosystem stability.

Keywords: Epidemiological Models, Population Dynamics, Stability Analysis, Bifurcation Theory, Reaction-Diffusion Systems.

Fractional Calculus & Non-local Modeling

Our research explores the power of fractional-order differential equations and other non-local operators to incorporate memory effects and long-range interactions inherent in many ecological and epidemiological systems, offering enhanced predictive accuracy.

Keywords: Fractional Derivatives, Memory Effects, Anomalous Diffusion, Complex Systems Ecology, Disease Persistence.

Advanced Stability & Control Theory

We employ rigorous analytical tools, including Lyapunov methodologies, spectral analysis, and optimal control theory, to investigate the stability of complex biological networks and to design effective intervention strategies for disease management and ecosystem preservation.

Keywords: Lyapunov Functions, Optimal Control, Network Stability, Intervention Strategies, Resilience.
